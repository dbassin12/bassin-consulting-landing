The Problem

Small businesses are caught in an AI adoption gap.

58%

of small businesses now use generative AI — but 51% are just experimenting without a clear path to real implementation.

62%

of non-adopters cite lack of understanding as their primary barrier to using AI in their business.

82%

of the smallest businesses (under 5 employees) believe AI simply doesn't apply to them.

The problem isn't a shortage of AI tools. It's a shortage of people who can explain those tools, implement them for a specific business context, and train the owner to maintain the result. Only 13% of AI projects make it from proof-of-concept to production. That gap is an education and implementation problem — and that's exactly what I solve.

How It Works

Three phases, from confusion to capability.

1
Diagnosis

A free 15-20 minute call to understand your business problem, assess whether AI is the right tool, and determine fit. This isn't a sales call — it's a teacher sitting down and asking, "Show me what's eating your time."

2
Build

We build the AI-powered solution together. You provide the domain expertise, I handle design and implementation. The iterative loop — you describe, I build, you test, I adjust — ensures the output actually fits your business.

3
Handoff

Training and documentation so you can maintain, adjust, and extend the tool independently. The goal is capability transfer, not recurring dependency. Every engagement ends with you understanding what was built and how to keep it running.
